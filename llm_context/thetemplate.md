# NotionBrain: Technical Template Description

## Overview
NotionBrain is a comprehensive personal organization system built in Notion, heavily inspired by the **Getting Things Done (GTD)** methodology. Its primary purpose is to maximize productivity and achieve total mental liberation by centralizing all life aspects into a single, high-alignment dashboard.

## 1. Structural Hierarchy (The Alignment Engine)
The system follows a top-down approach to ensure every daily action is connected to a larger purpose:
*   **Pillars:** The highest-level life areas (e.g., Health, Professional, Relationships, Finances).
*   **Objectives (OKRs):** Measurable goals derived directly from Pillars (e.g., "Exercise twice a week").
*   **Projects:** Specific workstreams required to reach an Objective (e.g., "Tennis Tournament").
*   **Tasks:** The smallest unit of work. Tasks can be linked to a Project, directly to an Objective, or exist as independent actions.

## 2. Capture & Processing Workflow
*   **Quick Action:** A streamlined entry point on the dashboard for instant capture of ideas or tasks, preventing focus disruption.
*   **Inbox (The Refinement Hub):** A dedicated staging area for unorganized entries. In this section, items are "refined" by assigning metadata: due dates, priority, project, objective, and pillar.

## 3. Execution & Visualization Views
*   **"Today" Section:** A focused view of tasks scheduled for the current day.
    *   **Simple List View:** For quick scanning and ticking.
    *   **Objective Grouping:** Provides a visual representation of how daily effort contributes to specific goals.
*   **Interactive Calendar:** Offers weekly and monthly overviews. It supports drag-and-drop functionality, allowing users to reschedule tasks by simply moving them across the grid, which automatically updates the database date.

## 4. Prioritization & Status Management
The system organizes pending items based on urgency and readiness:
*   **ASAP (As Soon As Possible):** High-priority tasks for immediate execution during available time slots.
*   **On Hold:** Tasks that are temporarily paused or awaiting external dependencies.
*   **Someday/Maybe:** A repository for future ideas and long-term aspirations that are not yet active.

## 5. Auxiliary Life Modules
Beyond the core productivity workflow, the template includes integrated spaces for holistic life management:
*   **Personal Journal:** For daily reflection and habit tracking.
*   **Knowledge Management:** Dedicated databases for Book Notes and Reading Lists.
*   **Personal Utilities:** Spaces for Recipes, Shopping Lists, and general reference notes.
